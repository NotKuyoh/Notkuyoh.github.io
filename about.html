About Me & My Project

When I moved into a larger home, I quickly realized the everyday tasks I had taken for granted were no longer as simple as they once were. Switching lights on and off, adjusting the temperature, or even monitoring the state of various devices around the house felt like a hassle. I wanted a smarter way to manage my home. Something that would make life easier, more connected, and more efficient. That's when the idea for my project, JOI™ (short for "Just One Intelligence"), was born — a voice assistant designed to manage all these tasks and more, all with the convenience of my voice.

Why I Started This Project

I started with a simple idea: create something that could help automate my home. But like many great ideas, this one began to evolve. As I dug deeper, I realized that existing smart home assistants came with a major downside: they were tied to large corporations. This meant I had little control over my own data. I wanted something more personal, something I could fully trust. That’s when I decided to build my own voice assistant, from the ground up, with privacy and independence at its core.

Learning & Development

At the very beginning, I had no experience in programming. But I didn’t let that stop me. I turned to the wealth of knowledge available online, using resources like ChatGPT, YouTube, and GitHub to learn Python. I bought a Raspberry Pi 3 Zero, an ESP32, and an Arduino, and started experimenting. My first successful project was controlling lights behind my TV using an LED strip and ESP32, which I programmed to run with the WLED software. The excitement of making something work got me hooked. But as I looked around my home, I realized there was more to be done. I wanted to control every light, every device, and every aspect of my home seamlessly. That’s when the idea of a Smart Voice Assistant started to take shape.

Challenges & Motivation

The journey from there was a steep learning curve. I started by creating a rule-based intent recognition system, but soon realized I needed something more advanced to make conversations feel natural. That’s when I decided to incorporate Llama, an AI model, to handle non-rule-based inputs and responses. JOI™ became something more than just a tool — she became an intelligent assistant that could interact with me in a more human way. I named her JOI™ after my friend, whose favorite movie is Blade Runner and whose favorite role is called JOI™. The name felt fitting, as it represented something both futuristic and personal.

Despite the many challenges I faced, including learning from scratch and solving technical issues, I remained motivated. Coding can be time-consuming, and the journey is rarely smooth. Bugs, errors, and unexpected obstacles are part of the process. But every time I fixed a problem or saw a project come to life, the sense of accomplishment fueled my drive. There was something incredibly satisfying about creating a fully functional assistant that could handle so many tasks.

Personal Use & Sharing the Code

JOI™ started as a personal project, something built for my own use. I didn’t initially plan to share the source code with the world, but as the project grew, I started to consider it. After all, the project’s journey could be helpful to others who are on the same path or looking to create their own smart home solutions. Still, I’ve yet to make that decision, as the project is very personal to me and I want to make sure it’s ready before opening it up to the public.

Future Vision

Looking forward, I have big plans for JOI™. My goal is to place the code on a running device that I can access from anywhere, allowing me to control my home no matter where I am. But that’s just the beginning. I’m also considering integrating JOI™ with smart glasses to bring the assistant’s features even closer to me, making it truly hands-free and seamless.

Conclusion

Creating JOI™ has been an incredible journey. From learning to code to developing something that truly makes life easier, it’s been both challenging and rewarding. I can’t wait to see where the project goes from here, and I’m excited about the future of smart, independent home automation.

Thank you for taking the time to read my story. I hope you find JOI™ as exciting and useful as I do!
